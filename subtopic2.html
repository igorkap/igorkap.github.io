 Computers in Education: A Brief History
Site Author
 Andrew Molnar
Abstract
 This site offers eleven pages of information related to the history of computers in education.  It starts out with the developments in the field of technology and cognitive science and moves on to the invention of the first computer.  After the brief introduction, it goes into great detail about the types of computers used in education and the important advancements of computers in education.  It talks about everything ranging from the very first computer- assisted teaching method to online degrees to project- oriented education.  The reader can learn all about the early pioneers of computer education, the timeline of computer education and why computers in education has made vast improvements in the field of not only education, but the economy, science, and technology.

 Using Computers in Schools
Site Author
 Eric Rusten
Abstract
 This site is actually a journal article written to address what can be done to utilize computers properly in education.  It especially focuses on bridging the information gap in less developed countries, such as Brazil.  Rusten explains that when computers are used effectively, they can be extremely helpful.  The can improve teaching and learning and strengthen school managment.  He describes four plans that he feels will enable computers and technology to reach the classroom.  They are Computer Assisted Learning (CAL), Simulation and Exploration, Research and Analysis, and Computation and Production.
